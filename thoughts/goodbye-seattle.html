Goodbye Seattle

Going where the wind takes me — and for now, it is carrying me back to the Bay.

2026-06-21 · Kasen Teoh

April 2018 was my first ever trip to Seattle. It was a family trip where we did all the touristy things. Pike Place, the Space Needle, and a short road trip to Vancouver. At the time, I thought Seattle was very beautiful: the cherry blossoms, the greenery, the waterfront views. It reminded me of San Francisco.

When the trip ended, I left wanting to explore the city more. I felt I didn't fully understand this place and wanted to return.

March 2022 when I graduated from UCSD, I was deciding on grad schools and UW was a solid choice. I decided to go on a trip to Seattle to visit the campus and some friends in the area.

This time, I wasn't too impressed by the Seattle metro area. Not to say I didn't have fun or didn't enjoy the nature, but just thought I enjoyed other aspects at that moment in my life. Maybe because I went to school in San Diego, which had consistently sunnier weather, beaches, and many more friends, and because I often traveled to LA for entertainment. So I eventually decided to attend UCLA for grad school.

April 2024 a really good friend of mine from grad school, let's call him X, moved to Bellevue to start his new job and got his H1B through the lottery on his first try. I was so happy and excited for him. I booked a flight to Seattle to catch up and celebrate with him.

August 2024 I decided to visit Seattle again because I was going through some personal matters and wanted a change of scenery. At the time another good friend of mine was also in Seattle to celebrate her birthday and I thought, perfect. We could have a small reunion with UCLA friends. This trip was particularly fun. We watched sunsets at Kerry Park, explored Everett and Snohomish, had good food and good memories.

January 2025 I decided on a whim that I wanted to move to Seattle. I wanted a change in my life. It had been 1.5 years since I moved back to the Bay Area to start working and I felt like I was going through the motions of life, just following a routine.

I chose Seattle because in terms of career it was very similar to the Bay, still surrounded by big tech but with the added bonus of no state income tax.

When I was packing my things getting ready to move, I was very torn between moving and staying in the Bay.

Staying. I grew up in the Bay so I had many more friends, was very familiar with the area, and my parents lived 10 minutes away. I was very comfortable in the Bay and my company's HQ was in Santa Clara.

Moving. I would get to experience living in a state outside of California. Having grown up in the Bay Area, going to undergrad in San Diego, and grad school in LA, I had a thorough tour of California and wanted to explore other states. I would be pushing myself to go out of my comfort zone. While the environment was similar to the Bay Area, I wasn't too familiar with Seattle and only had one close connection, X.

One week before moving, it finally hit me. I was so nervous and almost canceled everything. I told myself that yes I'm scared but it's something that would objectively be an experience I can grow from.

Enjoyable or not, I could learn a lot about building relationships outside of school and work. I could learn a lot about myself and explore a new place that for some reason kept pulling me back. And if it was an unpleasant experience, I could always go back, the Bay would always be my home.

March 22, 2025 was my official first day in Washington. I moved to Bellevue to an apartment a 5-minute walk from X's place. I got my first ever studio apartment, lived alone for the first time, and I was in a beautiful city embedded in a park right next to Lake Washington.

In Seattle, I forged new relationships through friends of friends, dating, and neighbors, and I reconnected with old friends from middle school who were also in the area. After a while, I suddenly had many more connections in Seattle than I thought I would ever have.

I also spent a lot of time alone, taking myself on solo dates and exploring new parks and restaurants. While others may think this is lonely, I never saw it that way. I never really had this much time to myself and I quite enjoyed it. I could sit with my thoughts, reflect more, and just let my mind wander.

I'm not even sure how to describe my experience in Seattle. It was definitely slower than my life in the Bay, and much closer to nature. Taking pictures of Rainier whenever the weather is clear enough, walking in Bellevue next to $10 million plus homes, watching sunsets in Kerry Park, the first breath of refreshing oxygen as soon as you step out of SEA. I loved it all. Seattle started to feel like a home.

June 2026 I got a new job in the Bay Area, so it was time for me to move back. Many of my friends ask if I'm excited to be back in the Bay. I definitely am. I have missed my friends, my connections, the fast pace. At the same time, I am sad to be leaving Seattle. Here I have grown as a person, learned a lot, and built a life.

For years, Seattle kept pulling me back and I could never really explain why. Maybe this was the reason all along, these 15 months.

I have been saying going where the wind takes me. For now, the wind is taking me back to the Bay Area. Not to say I will not be back in Seattle, but this experience has meant a lot and is coming to an end.

To the new and old relationships I built, maintained, and reconnected with, I wholeheartedly thank you for making this journey very enjoyable.
